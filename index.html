Biblionasium's Safe and Effective Platform is the Perfect Environment for Teaching Digital Citizenship

With virtual learning on the rise, learning how to be a good digital citizen online is more important than ever. In addition to providing written materials, a full presentation, and a short, engaging video for students as part of our free digital citizenship curriculum, Biblionasium provides a hands-on opportunity for kids to learn digital citizenship in a safe and controlled social network environment.

Biblionasium is the largest COPPA certified digital social reading platform for kids. As such, our commitment to student safety and privacy is paramount. We have designed our platform to eliminate the risk of encountering strangers, and everything from account creation to participating in groups to reading and writing book reviews is controlled by teachers and parents. This makes Biblionasium the perfect setting to safely learn real world applications for digital citizenship.

Our full digital citizenship program includes:

  • A written lesson plan for teachers
  • A presentation for delivering the program to students in a classroom setting
  • A short video (less than 2 minutes) to give a quick, simple definition of digital citizenship
  • Printable digital citizenship activities for students (including examples of good book recommendations and reviews)
  • Printable digital citizenship pledge to sign at the end of the lesson

Elements of the Curriculum

Lesson Plan for Educators

Our program includes a full lesson plan for teachers and a presentation. The presentation is downloadable in PowerPoint or PDF form.


Printable Activities

In our printable exercises, students will identify book recommendations and book reviews that are "most helpful" and "least helpful" to their classmates.


Digital Citizenship Pledge

Our digital citizenship pledge asks students to adhere to our Honor Code and practice good digital citizenship on the platform.
